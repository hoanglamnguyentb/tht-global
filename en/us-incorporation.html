1. Can a foreigner register a company in the U.S?

Foreign individuals/companies have the opportunity to register a company in the US with full ownership rights. Local residents and foreigners can legally own 100% of the US company.

2. What kinds of business are allowed in USA?

The United States allows a wide range of business activities across various industries. Common types of business activities permitted in the USA include: Professional Services, Retail and E-commerce, Technology and Software Development, Manufacturing and Production and more.

3. How many types of Coporations are there in the US?

In the United States, there are several types of corporations that businesses can choose to form. The most common types of corporations include:

  • C-Corp
  • S-Corp
  • B corp (Benefit Corporation)
  • Close corporation
  • Nonprofit corporation

To ensure seamless cash flow and uninterrupted business operations, we highly recommend diversifying your corporate bank accounts with at least two types: digital and physical. This strategic approach mitigates the risk of unexpected cash flow disruptions, such as bank account freezes or other unforeseen circumstances.

3. What is a digital bank account? Why you should open one?

A digital bank account is created by digital banks (They offer many of the same banking services as a traditional bank, but the main difference is that digital banks don't have physical branches). The benefits of opening one is its fast and simple account registration, cost efficiency and time saving and the ability to use immediately once opened.
